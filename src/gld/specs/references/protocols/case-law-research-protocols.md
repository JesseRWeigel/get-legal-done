# Case Law Research Protocols

> Step-by-step methodology guides for case law research across practice areas.

## Protocol: Federal Constitutional Law Research

### When to Use
Issues involving constitutional rights, due process, equal protection, First Amendment.

### Steps
1. **Identify the constitutional provision** — which amendment, which clause
2. **Determine the level of scrutiny** — strict, intermediate, rational basis
3. **Find the controlling Supreme Court case** — most recent definitive ruling
4. **Trace the doctrine** — how has the Supreme Court developed this area?
5. **Check circuit-level application** — how has your circuit applied the doctrine?
6. **Search for distinguishing facts** — cases with similar facts to yours
7. **Identify any circuit splits** — are circuits divided on this issue?
8. **Check for pending certiorari** — is the Supreme Court about to change the law?
9. **Search for adverse authority** — strongest cases against your position

### Common LLM Pitfalls
- Citing overruled constitutional precedent (L003) — constitutional law changes frequently
- Confusing the level of scrutiny (L007/L013)
- Citing dicta from concurrences as holding (L010/L011)
- Missing recent doctrinal shifts

## Protocol: Contract Dispute Research

### When to Use
Breach of contract, contract interpretation, formation, defenses.

### Steps
1. **Identify governing law** — choice of law clause? UCC or common law?
2. **Determine the type of contract** — goods (UCC Article 2), services, real property
3. **Research elements of breach** — formation, performance, breach, damages
4. **Check contract interpretation rules** — plain meaning, parol evidence, integration
5. **Research available defenses** — statute of frauds, unconscionability, impossibility, frustration
6. **Search for cases with similar contract language** — courts interpreting similar terms
7. **Research damages** — expectation, reliance, restitution, consequential, mitigation
8. **Check for applicable Restatement sections** — Restatement (Second) of Contracts

### Common LLM Pitfalls
- Confusing UCC and common law standards (L013)
- Applying the wrong state's contract law (L005)
- Citing superseded UCC provisions (L006)

## Protocol: Tort Litigation Research

### When to Use
Negligence, intentional torts, strict liability, products liability.

### Steps
1. **Identify the tort theory** — negligence, intentional, strict liability
2. **Research elements** — duty, breach, causation, damages (for negligence)
3. **Determine the duty standard** — reasonable person, professional standard, landowner duties
4. **Research causation** — but-for, substantial factor, proximate cause, foreseeability
5. **Check for comparative/contributory negligence** — which system applies in your jurisdiction?
6. **Research available defenses** — assumption of risk, statute of limitations, immunity
7. **Search for cases with similar fact patterns** — key: how courts handle similar scenarios
8. **Research damages caps** — some jurisdictions cap non-economic or punitive damages
9. **Check for applicable Restatement sections** — Restatement (Third) of Torts

### Common LLM Pitfalls
- Applying the wrong negligence standard for the jurisdiction (L005)
- Confusing comparative and contributory negligence systems (L013)
- Citing pre-reform tort cases in reformed jurisdictions (L003)

## Protocol: Administrative Law Research

### When to Use
Agency rulemaking, administrative adjudication, judicial review of agency action.

### Steps
1. **Identify the agency and enabling statute** — what authority does the agency have?
2. **Determine the type of agency action** — rulemaking, adjudication, informal guidance
3. **Research the standard of judicial review** — Chevron (if still applicable), Skidmore, arbitrary and capricious, substantial evidence
4. **Check for recent changes** — administrative law doctrine is evolving rapidly (Loper Bright)
5. **Search the Federal Register** — rulemaking history, comments, final rules
6. **Check the CFR** — current regulatory text
7. **Research exhaustion and ripeness** — has the plaintiff exhausted administrative remedies?
8. **Search for circuit-level interpretations** — how has your circuit applied the standard?

### Common LLM Pitfalls
- Applying outdated Chevron deference framework (L003/L006) — doctrine may be modified or overruled
- Confusing different standards of review for different types of agency action (L007)
- Citing superseded regulations (L006)
- Missing recent Supreme Court changes to administrative law doctrine

## Protocol: Civil Rights / Section 1983 Research

### When to Use
Claims under 42 U.S.C. 1983, constitutional violations by state actors.

### Steps
1. **Identify the constitutional right** — which amendment?
2. **Verify state action** — is the defendant a state actor?
3. **Research qualified immunity** — clearly established law at the time of the violation
4. **Check for municipal liability** — Monell doctrine (policy, custom, deliberate indifference)
5. **Research the specific right** — Fourth Amendment (excessive force), Fourteenth Amendment (due process), First Amendment (retaliation)
6. **Search for circuit-specific standards** — circuits differ on qualified immunity analysis
7. **Research damages** — compensatory, punitive, nominal, attorneys' fees (42 U.S.C. 1988)
8. **Check for recent qualified immunity decisions** — this is a rapidly evolving area

### Common LLM Pitfalls
- Applying the wrong qualified immunity test for the circuit (L005)
- Citing pre-2020 qualified immunity decisions without checking for doctrinal changes (L003)
- Confusing individual and municipal liability standards (L013)
